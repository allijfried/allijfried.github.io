The Bushido School of Karate

Bushido School of Karate wanted to revamp their website. For the user, finding information and navigating content was a challenge. My goal was to reorganize the existing content, which was comprehensive and complete already, into a clear and user-friendly interface. Below are some shots of the original site's design.

Site Map

Several issues needed to be addressed. I needed to make the site, with its unusually high volume of varied content, feel less overwhelming to the user. Therefore, I had to completely reorganize the website. Additionally, the call to action (CTA) needed to be more clear. On each page that described a “class type” there would be an obvious CTA. Finally, we had to provide quick load times and easy usability.

Navigation Exploration

The ideal target audiences for Bushido School of Karate are fitness-oriented adults of all ages, as well as parents with children of any age. Because of the wide age range of potential users, I had to make sure the navigation was very clearly laid out and simple to use. Below are examples of some of the navigation options explored.

Final Choice for Navigation

Ultimately, given the wide age-range of the target audience, I needed to employ a more simplified navigation layout to help the user more easily understand the site’s flow.

As the user scrolls down the page, the navigation condenses to take up less real estate.
