The Bushido School of Karate

Freelance Designer

In early 2016, Bushido School of Karate approached me to redesign their website. Their old site was made in the 1990s and therefore lacks the visual joy that most modern businesses attempt to bring to their online presence. This project is still in progress and is currently under active development.

My responsibilities in the project include:

Reorganization of the old content into a set of new pages. I planned this out by creating a sitemap.
UI for navigation among the various informational pages
Production of high fidelity mocks

Several issues needed to be addressed. I needed to make the site, with its unusually high volume of varied content, feel less overwhelming to the user. Therefore, I had to completely reorganize the website. Additionally, their call to action (CTA) needed to be more clear. They needed to make it easy for new and returning clients to stay informed and know how to sign up for classes. On each page that described a “class type” there would be an obvious CTA. Finally, we had to provide quick load times and easy usability.

Navigation Exploration

The ideal target audiences for Bushido School of Karate are fitness-oriented adults of all ages, as well as parents with children of any age. Because of the wide age range of potential users, I had to make sure the navigation was very clearly laid out and simple to use. Below are examples of some of the navigation options explored.
